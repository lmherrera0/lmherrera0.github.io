This paper proposes a hallucination governance architecture characterised by five interconnected components that provide the integrated verification governance the current landscape lacks. Five-Component Architecture

1

Calibrated Source Hierarchy

Four-tier source hierarchy defined by epistemological criteria ‚Äî institutional backing, peer corroboration, methodological transparency ‚Äî with five colour-coded verification markers enabling independent human validation.

2

Adversarial Quality Gate

Quantified risk scoring across eight dimensions (scored 1‚Äì3, max 24) with cognitive bias detection and logical fallacy screening.

3

Dual-Mode Execution

Fast Mode for everyday governance and Deep Audit for high-stakes deliverables ‚Äî same verification standard, adaptive depth.

4

Channel-Agnostic Governance

Universal verification rules separated from domain-specific templates across 22 professional communication channels.

5

Cross-Platform Deployment

Platform adapters for Claude, ChatGPT, Google Gems, and Microsoft Copilot ensuring consistent governance across systems.

Key Paper Features Implemented in GGP

Tree of Thought

Explores 2‚Äì3 alternative resolution paths in parallel for each inference or gap. Evaluates consequences against audience sensitivity, channel risk, and regulatory exposure. Ranks by defensibility.

Cognitive Bias Detection

Screens for 4 biases: Confirmation, Survivorship, Status Quo, and Recency. Named biases are flagged with actionable markers, not vague warnings.

Logical Risk Patterns

Detects 4 fallacies: False Dilemma, Hasty Generalisation, False Causality, and Non Sequitur. Each pattern triggers a specific rewrite recommendation.

Theoretical Foundations

Accountability Theory

Every claim traceable, every verification step auditable (Bovens, 2007)

Epistemology of Testimony

Trust calibrated by institutional backing, peer corroboration, and methodological transparency (Coady, 1992; Lackey, 2008)

Information Governance

Quality management embedded in the production workflow, not post-hoc review (Smallwood, 2014)

Core design principle: verification is a workflow property, not a model property.
